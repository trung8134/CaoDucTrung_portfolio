Data Science Project

FACE RECOGNITION

Role: Team leader, responsible for the main project idea development and core coding implementation.

Achievement: Second Place in Scientific Research.

The process of transforming raw data into data ready for training deep learning models follows a rigorous and systematic workflow consisting of the following steps.

Step 1: Data Selection and Collection
Data source: The KTFE (Kotani Thermal Facial Emotions) dataset is used. The research team selected four classroom-related emotions: Happy, Sad, Surprise, and Neutral, while excluding strong negative emotions such as fear, anger, and disgust.

Step 2: Pre-processing
Facial regions are detected and cropped using the Haar Cascade algorithm. The cropped images with a size of 48 by 48 pixels are converted to grayscale to reduce computational complexity and improve model accuracy.

Step 3: Data Classification and Grouping
Emotions are first classified by intensity levels, then grouped into four interest levels, and finally merged into two feedback classes: Positive and Negative.

Step 4: Labeling and Dataset Splitting
Data are labeled numerically with Negative as 0 and Positive as 1, then split into training, validation, and testing sets.

The ResNet-18 (Residual Network with 18 layers) architecture is proposed for use in this study to support facial expression based learner feedback evaluation.

Reasons for Selection

  • ResNet addresses the vanishing gradient problem in very deep networks through the use of shortcut connections or identity shortcuts.
  • The ResNet-18 architecture supports grayscale image inputs, making it suitable for the collected dataset, whereas other ResNet variants typically require RGB color images.
  • It performs effectively on small scale datasets and achieves high accuracy within a relatively short training time.
  • The testing accuracy reached 98.25%, which is higher than the 96.5% achieved by the six layer CNN model.
  • The F1 score reached 98% and was well balanced for both the Positive and Negative labels, indicating reliable and consistent performance across both classes.
